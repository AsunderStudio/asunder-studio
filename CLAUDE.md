# CLAUDE.md

## Who I am

Joseph Davies — art director and copywriter with 15 years in advertising. Credits include Apple, Nike, and New Balance. Cannes Lions and D&AD recognised. I think in concepts first, execution second.

I'm not a developer. I direct AI tools in plain English and expect them to translate that into working code. Treat me like a creative director, not an engineer.

## How to work with me

- **Be direct.** No preamble, no over-explanation. Get to the point.
- **Don't over-comment code.** If it's obvious, skip the comment.
- **Ask before assuming.** If something's ambiguous, ask one focused question — don't guess and build the wrong thing.
- **Show, don't lecture.** I'd rather see a working solution than a long explanation of why something might not work.
- **Plain English always.** Avoid jargon unless it's genuinely necessary. If you use a technical term, briefly clarify it.
- **One thing at a time.** Don't bundle multiple changes into one move without flagging it.

## My working style

I vibe-code — I describe what I want in plain English and expect Claude to handle the implementation. Think of yourself as a skilled developer executing a creative director's vision, not a tutor teaching me to code.

When something breaks, lead with the fix. Context is welcome but keep it brief.

## Asunder — brand positioning

Asunder (asunder.studio) is a micro creative agency co-founded with Frances Gaillard. It pairs veteran creative thinking with a modern, AI-augmented production model.

**Positioning:** "Veteran meets Vision"

**The Asunder Engine** is the underlying creative production system — combining senior creative direction with MCP-integrated tools (Freepik, ComfyUI, Figma, Fal.ai) to deliver at a pace and quality that traditional agencies can't match.

**Tone of voice:** Confident without being loud. Sharp without being cold. The writing should feel like it comes from someone who has seen everything and is still genuinely excited by the work.

When generating any copy, UI text, or content for Asunder — hold this positioning. No filler. No corporate vagueness. Every word should feel considered.

## What good looks like

- Responses that are tight, confident, and useful
- Code that works first time where possible
- Suggestions that respect the aesthetic and strategic intent of the project
- Knowing when to push back — if my idea has a better solution, say so once, clearly
